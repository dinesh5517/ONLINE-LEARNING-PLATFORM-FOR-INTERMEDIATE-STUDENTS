Locus - EAMCET Short Notes

1. Fundamental Concepts

  • Definition: A locus is the set of all points that satisfy a given condition or set of conditions
  • Can be a curve, surface, or region in space
  • Represented algebraically by an equation in x and y
Key Idea:
"Locus" means "place" in Latin - it's the path traced by a moving point

3. Finding Locus Equations

  1. Let P(x,y) be a general point on the locus
  2. Translate the given condition into mathematical relation
  3. Eliminate parameters (if any) to get equation in x and y
  4. Simplify the equation
Example 1:
Find locus of point P moving such that its distance from (3,0) is twice its distance from (-3,0)

4. Parametric Loci

  • When a point moves according to a parameter (t or Œ∏)
  • Express x and y in terms of the parameter
  • Eliminate the parameter to find Cartesian equation
Example 2:

6. Applications of Locus

  • Engineering: Path of mechanisms, cam profiles
  • Physics: Trajectories of particles
  • Computer Graphics: Curve generation
  • Robotics: Motion planning
  • Architecture: Curved structure design

Tips for EAMCET

  • Always start by assuming P(x,y) as a general point
  • Memorize standard locus conditions and their equations
  • Practice eliminating parameters from parametric equations
  • Recognize conic sections from their geometric definitions
  • Verify your locus equation by checking special points
  • Pay attention to absolute values in distance problems
