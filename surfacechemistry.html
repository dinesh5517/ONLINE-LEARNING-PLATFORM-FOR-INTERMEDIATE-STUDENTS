Surface Chemistry - EAMCET Short Notes

1. Introduction to Surface Chemistry

  • Surface chemistry is the study of chemical phenomena that occur at the interface of two phases.
  • It deals with concepts like adsorption, catalysis, colloids, and emulsions.
  • Surface chemistry has numerous applications in industry, medicine, and daily life.
  • Key processes include:
    • Adsorption (solid-gas and solid-liquid interfaces)
    • Catalysis (heterogeneous and homogeneous)
    • Formation and properties of colloids
    • Emulsification and emulsion stability

2. Adsorption

  • Definition: The phenomenon of accumulation of molecules of a substance on the surface of a solid or liquid.
  • Adsorbate: The substance that gets adsorbed
  • Adsorbent: The substance on whose surface adsorption occurs
  • Types of Adsorption:
    • Physical Adsorption (Physisorption): Involves weak van der Waals forces
    • Chemical Adsorption (Chemisorption): Involves formation of chemical bonds
Physical Adsorption Chemical Adsorption
Weak van der Waals forces Strong chemical bonds
Low heat of adsorption (20-40 kJ/mol) High heat of adsorption (80-240 kJ/mol)

3. Adsorption Isotherms

  • Adsorption isotherms describe the relationship between the amount of gas adsorbed and pressure at constant temperature.
  • Freundlich Adsorption Isotherm:
    x/m = kP1/n
  • Where x/m is amount adsorbed per unit mass, P is pressure, k and n are constants
  • In log form: log(x/m) = log k + (1/n)log P
  • Langmuir Adsorption Isotherm:
    x/m = (abP)/(1 + bP)
  • Where a and b are constants related to adsorption capacity and energy
  • Assumes monolayer adsorption on homogeneous surfaces
  • BET Theory: Extension of Langmuir isotherm for multilayer adsorption

4. Catalysis

  • Definition: A catalyst is a substance that increases the rate of a reaction without being consumed in the reaction.
  • Types of Catalysis:
    • Homogeneous Catalysis: Catalyst and reactants are in the same phase
    • Heterogeneous Catalysis: Catalyst and reactants are in different phases
    • Enzyme Catalysis: Catalysis by biological enzymes
  • Characteristics of Catalysts:
    • Small amounts are sufficient
    • Remain unchanged in mass and chemical composition
    • Cannot initiate a reaction
    • Highly specific in action
    • Can be inhibited (poisoned) by certain substances
  • Mechanism of Heterogeneous Catalysis:
    • Adsorption of reactants on catalyst surface
    • Formation of activated complex
    • Rearrangement of atoms
    • Desorption of products

5. Colloids

  • Definition: Heterogeneous mixtures where one substance is dispersed (dispersed phase) in another (dispersion medium).
  • Particle size ranges from 1 nm to 1000 nm (1 Î¼m)
  • Types of Colloids:

6. Properties of Colloids

  • Tyndall Effect: Scattering of light by colloidal particles
  • Brownian Movement: Random zigzag movement of colloidal particles
  • Electrophoresis: Movement of colloidal particles under the influence of an electric field
  • Coagulation/Flocculation: Process of settling down of colloidal particles
  • Dialysis: Removal of dissolved substances from a colloidal solution
  • Electro-osmosis: Movement of dispersion medium under the influence of an electric field

7. Preparation of Colloids

  • Dispersion Methods: Breaking larger particles into colloidal size
    • Mechanical dispersion
    • Electro-dispersion (Bredig's Arc method)
    • Ultrasonic dispersion
    • Peptization
  • Condensation Methods: Aggregation of smaller particles to colloidal size
    • Chemical methods (oxidation, reduction, hydrolysis, double decomposition)
    • Exchange of solvent
    • Excessive cooling

8. Purification of Colloids

  • Dialysis: Removal of impurities by diffusion through a semipermeable membrane
  • Electro-dialysis: Dialysis accelerated by applying an electric field
  • Ultrafiltration: Filtration under pressure through special filters

9. Emulsions

  • Definition: Liquid-liquid colloidal systems where one liquid is dispersed in another immiscible liquid.
  • Types:
    • Oil-in-Water (O/W): Oil droplets dispersed in water (e.g., milk, vanishing cream)
    • Water-in-Oil (W/O): Water droplets dispersed in oil (e.g., butter, cod liver oil)
  • Emulsifiers: Substances that stabilize emulsions by forming a film around dispersed droplets (e.g., soap, detergents, proteins)

Tips for EAMCET

  • Understand the differences between physical and chemical adsorption thoroughly.
  • Know how to apply adsorption isotherms (Freundlich and Langmuir) to solve numerical problems.
  • Remember the classification of colloids based on dispersed phase and dispersing medium.
  • Focus on properties of colloids like Tyndall effect, Brownian movement, and electrophoresis.
  • Understand the mechanisms of catalysis, especially heterogeneous catalysis.
  • Learn about preparation methods for colloids and how to distinguish between them.
  • Practice problems related to adsorption isotherms and Hardy-Schulze rule.
  • Know how to identify different types of emulsions and their applications.
