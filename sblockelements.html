1. Alkali Metals (Group 1)

  • Elements: Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, Fr
  • Electronic Configuration: [Noble gas] ns1
  • Properties:
    • Soft, silvery-white metals
    • Low melting points
    • Highly reactive (stored in oil)
    • Strong reducing agents
  • Trends:
    • Atomic size increases down the group
    • Ionization energy decreases
    • Reactivity increases

2. Alkaline Earth Metals (Group 2)

  • Elements: Be, Mg, Ca, Sr, Ba, Ra
  • Electronic Configuration: [Noble gas] ns2
  • Properties:
    • Harder than alkali metals
    • Higher melting points
    • Less reactive than Group 1
    • Form divalent cations
  • Trends:
    • Atomic size increases down the group
    • Ionization energy decreases
    • Solubility of hydroxides increases

EAMCET Problem-Solving Tips

  • Remember flame test colors (use mnemonic: "LiNa Ki Ruby Cse Baar" for Li-Red, Na-Yellow, K-Lilac)
  • For solubility trends:
    • Hydroxides: Increases down Group 1 and 2
    • Sulfates: Decreases down Group 2
    • Carbonates: Insoluble except Group 1
  • Important industrial processes:
    • Solvay process (Na2CO3)
    • Down's process (Na metal)
    • Chlor-alkali process (NaOH)
  • Anomalous behavior:
