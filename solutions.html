6. Anomalous Behavior of Solutions

  • Negative Deviation from Raoult's Law:
    • Occurs when solute-solvent interactions are stronger than solute-solute and solvent-solvent interactions
    • Examples: Acetone-chloroform, HCl-water
  • Positive Deviation from Raoult's Law:
    • Occurs when solute-solvent interactions are weaker than solute-solute and solvent-solvent interactions
    • Examples: Ethanol-cyclohexane, benzene-methanol
  • Azeotropic Mixtures:
    • Mixtures that have same composition in liquid and vapor phases
    • Cannot be separated by simple distillation
    • Minimum boiling azeotropes (positive deviation): Ethanol-water (95.6% ethanol)
    • Maximum boiling azeotropes (negative deviation): HCl-water

7. Applications of Colligative Properties

  • Determination of Molar Mass:
    • Using freezing point depression
    • Using boiling point elevation
    • Using osmotic pressure (most accurate)
  • Isotonic Solutions: Solutions having same osmotic pressure
  • Reverse Osmosis: Water purification technique used in desalination
  • Antifreeze: Ethylene glycol in car radiators (freezing point depression)
  • Measurement of degree of dissociation of weak electrolytes
