Basic Concepts of Chemistry - EAMCET Short Notes

1. Matter and Its Classification

  • Matter: Anything that occupies space and has mass
  • States of Matter: Solid, Liquid, Gas (and Plasma)
  • Classification Based on Composition:
    • Pure Substances: Elements and Compounds
    • Mixtures: Homogeneous and Heterogeneous

  • Subatomic Particles:

  • Mass Number (A): Protons + Neutrons

  • Isobars: Same A, different Z
  • Bohr's Model: Electrons revolve in fixed energy levels

5. Stoichiometry and Chemical Calculations

  • Stoichiometry: Quantitative relationship in chemical reactions
  • Percentage Composition: % of each element by mass
  • Limiting Reagent: Reactant that gets consumed first
  • Theoretical Yield: Maximum amount of product possible
  • Actual Yield: Amount actually produced in lab
  • Percent Yield: (Actual/Theoretical) √ó 100%

  • Concentration Units:

Tips for EAMCET

  • Practice unit conversions and dimensional analysis
  • Memorize common polyatomic ions and their charges
  • Be familiar with mole concept calculations and balancing equations
  • Remember the differences between empirical vs. molecular formulas
  • Practice limiting reagent problems with multiple-step calculations
  • Learn to interconvert between concentration units
  • Memorize atomic weights of common elements (H, C, N, O, Na, Mg, Al, S, Cl, K, Ca, Fe)
