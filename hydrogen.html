Hydrogen - EAMCET Short Notes

1. Position of Hydrogen in Periodic Table

  • Unique Position: Placed in both Group 1 (alkali metals) and Group 17 (halogens)
  • Similarities with Alkali Metals:
    • Electronic configuration: ns1
    • Forms H+ ions
    • Forms oxides, halides, and sulfides
  • Similarities with Halogens:
    • Diatomic molecule (H2)
    • Forms H- ions (hydrides)
    • High ionization enthalpy

  • Tritium (3H or T): 2 neutrons, radioactive (Î²-emitter)

EAMCET Problem-Solving Tips

  • Remember hydrogen's dual nature (alkali metal + halogen properties)
  • For isotope problems:
    • Protium has no neutrons
    • Heavy water contains deuterium
    • Tritium is radioactive
  • Hydride formation trends:
    • Alkali metals form ionic hydrides (MH)
    • Alkaline earth metals form ionic hydrides (MH2)
    • Non-metals form covalent hydrides
  • Hydrogen peroxide acts as both oxidizing and reducing agent
  • Memorize important reactions:
    • Water gas reaction: C + H2O â†’ CO + H2
    • Bosch process: CO + H2O â†’ CO2 + H2
