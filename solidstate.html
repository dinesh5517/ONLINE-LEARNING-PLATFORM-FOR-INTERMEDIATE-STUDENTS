Solid State - EAMCET Short Notes

1. Classification of Solids

Type Properties Examples
Crystalline Long-range order, sharp melting point NaCl, Diamond
Amorphous Short-range order, no sharp melting point Glass, Rubber

2. Crystal Lattices and Unit Cells

  • Unit Cell: Smallest repeating unit of crystal lattice
  • Bravais Lattices: 14 possible arrangements in 3D
  • Cubic Systems:
    • Simple cubic (1 atom/unit cell)
    • Body-centered cubic (bcc, 2 atoms)
    • Face-centered cubic (fcc, 4 atoms)
    • Packing Efficiency:
    Simple cubic: 52% ‚Ä¢ bcc: 68% ‚Ä¢ fcc/hcp: 74%

3. Crystal Defects

  • Point Defects:
    • Vacancy (missing atom)
    • Interstitial (extra atom)
    • Schottky (cation+anion vacancy)
    • Frenkel (displaced ion)
  • Impurity Defects: Doping (e.g., As in Si)
  • Non-stoichiometric Defects: Metal excess (F-centers) or deficiency

4. Electrical Properties

Type Conduction Examples
Conductors Free electrons Metals (Cu, Ag)
Insulators No free electrons Diamond, Sulphur
Semiconductors Intermediate Si, Ge
  • n-type: Extra electrons (As in Si)
  • p-type: Electron holes (B in Si)

7. Important Structures

  • NaCl: fcc, 6:6 coordination, 4 formula units/unit cell
  • ZnS: Tetrahedral holes, 4:4 coordination
  • CsCl: Simple cubic, 8:8 coordination
  • Diamond: fcc, tetrahedral, 8 atoms/unit cell

Tips for EAMCET

  • Memorize coordination numbers of common structures
  • Practice density calculations using unit cell parameters
  • Understand the difference between various crystal defects
  • Learn to identify n-type vs p-type semiconductors
  • Focus on packing efficiency calculations
  • Remember examples for each magnetic property type
