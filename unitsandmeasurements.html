1. Physical Quantity

A quantity that can be measured is called a physical quantity.

Examples: length, mass, time, temperature.

2. Units

A unit is a standard used to measure a physical quantity.

Examples: meter (m), kilogram (kg), second (s).

3. Types of Units

Fundamental Units: Cannot be broken down (e.g., meter, kilogram, second).

Derived Units: Formed from fundamental units (e.g., m/s, N, J).

4. System of Units

CGS: centimeter, gram, second

MKS: meter, kilogram, second

SI: International system (7 base units)

6. Accuracy, Precision, and Errors

Accuracy: Closeness to the true value.

Precision: Closeness of repeated measurements.

Errors: Difference from true value.

Types: Systematic, Random, Gross.

7. Significant Figures

  • All non-zero digits are significant.
  • Zeros between significant digits are also significant.

8. Dimensions and Dimensional Formula

Dimension: Nature of a physical quantity.

Formula: Expressed in terms of base quantities (e.g., Force = M1L1T-2).

9. Applications of Dimensional Analysis

  • To convert units
  • To verify equations
  • To derive relationships
