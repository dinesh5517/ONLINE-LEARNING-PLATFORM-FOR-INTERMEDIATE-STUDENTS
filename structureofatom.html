Atomic Structure - EAMCET Detailed Notes

1. Historical Development of Atomic Theory

  • Dalton's Atomic Theory (1808):
    • Matter consists of indivisible atoms
    • Atoms of same element are identical
    • Compounds form when atoms combine in simple ratios
    • Chemical reactions involve rearrangement of atoms
  • Thomson's Model (1897):
    • Discovered electrons using cathode ray experiments
    • Proposed "Plum Pudding Model" ‚Äì negative electrons embedded in positive sphere
  • Rutherford's Model (1911):
    • Gold foil experiment ‚Äì discovered nucleus
    • Most of atom is empty space with dense, positive nucleus
    • Electrons orbit the nucleus like planets around sun
  • Bohr's Model (1913):
    • Electrons move in fixed circular orbits (energy levels)
    • Each orbit has fixed energy (quantized)
    • Electrons emit/absorb energy when jumping between orbits
  • Modern Quantum Model:
    • Electrons exist in electron clouds (orbitals)
    • Position and momentum cannot be precisely determined (Heisenberg's uncertainty principle)
    • Electrons described by wave functions (probability distributions)

3. Fundamental Concepts

  • Atomic Number (Z): Number of protons in nucleus
    Z = Number of protons = Number of electrons (in neutral atom)
  • Mass Number (A): Total number of nucleons (protons + neutrons)
    A = Number of protons + Number of neutrons
  • Notation: AZX (where X is element symbol)
    Example: 168O means oxygen with 8 protons and 8 neutrons
  • Isotopes: Atoms with same Z but different neutron count (different A)
    Examples: 11H, 21H (deuterium), 31H (tritium) - all hydrogen isotopes
  • Isobars: Atoms of different elements with same A but different Z
    Examples: 4020Ca and 4019K - both have mass number 40
  • Isotones: Atoms with same number of neutrons but different Z values
    Examples: 136C and 147N - both have 7 neutrons
  • Isoelectronic Species: Atoms/ions with same number of electrons
    Examples: F‚Åª, Ne, Na‚Å∫, Mg¬≤‚Å∫ - all have 10 electrons

6. Electron Configuration

  • Electronic Configuration: Arrangement of electrons in orbitals
  • Aufbau Principle: Electrons fill lowest energy orbitals first
  • Pauli's Exclusion Principle: No two electrons can have all four quantum numbers the same
  • Hund's Rule: Electrons occupy degenerate orbitals singly before pairing

7. Atomic Properties

Atomic Radius

  • Definition: Half the distance between nuclei of two adjacent atoms
  • Types: Covalent radius, Van der Waals radius, Metallic radius, Ionic radius
  • Trends:
    • Decreases across period (left to right)
    • Increases down group (top to bottom)
  • Reason: Across period ‚Üí Nuclear charge increases ‚Üí Greater pull on electrons
    Down group ‚Üí New shells added ‚Üí Greater distance from nucleus

Ionization Energy

  • Definition: Energy required to remove an electron from gaseous atom
  • First Ionization Energy: Energy to remove 1st electron
  • Second Ionization Energy: Energy to remove 2nd electron (always higher)
  • Trends:
    • Increases across period (left to right)
    • Decreases down group (top to bottom)
  • Exceptions: Be > B and N > O in first ionization energy
  • Reason: Across period ‚Üí Nuclear charge increases ‚Üí Stronger attraction
    Down group ‚Üí Distance increases ‚Üí Weaker attraction

Electron Affinity

  • Definition: Energy released when an electron is added to a gaseous atom
  • Trends:
    • Generally increases across period (left to right)
    • Generally decreases down group (top to bottom)
  • Highest: Halogens (Group 17)
  • Lowest: Noble gases (Group 18) - very low or negative values

Electronegativity

  • Definition: Tendency of an atom to attract shared electrons in a bond
  • Pauling Scale: Most common scale (F = 4.0, highest)
  • Trends:
    • Increases across period (left to right)
    • Decreases down group (top to bottom)
  • Highest: F > O > N > Cl
  • Lowest: Alkali metals (Group 1)

9. Electronic Configurations and the Periodic Table

  • Blocks of the Periodic Table:
    • s-block: Groups 1 and 2 (filling of s-orbitals)
    • p-block: Groups 13 to 18 (filling of p-orbitals)
    • d-block: Groups 3 to 12 (filling of d-orbitals)
    • f-block: Lanthanides and Actinides (filling of f-orbitals)
  • Valence Electrons: Electrons in outermost shell
  • Octet Rule: Atoms tend to gain, lose, or share electrons to get 8 valence electrons
  • Noble Gas Configuration: Full outer shell (stable configuration)
  • Electronic Configuration and Periodicity:
    • Group 1 (Alkali metals): ns¬π
    • Group 2 (Alkaline earth metals): ns¬≤
    • Group 13 (Boron group): ns¬≤ np¬π
    • Group 14 (Carbon group): ns¬≤ np¬≤
    • Group 15 (Pnictogens): ns¬≤ np¬≥
