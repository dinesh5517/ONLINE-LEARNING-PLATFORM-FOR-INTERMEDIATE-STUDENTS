Electrochemistry - EAMCET Short Notes

1. Introduction to Electrochemistry

  • Electrochemistry is the branch of chemistry that deals with the relationship between electricity and chemical reactions.
  • It involves the study of chemical changes caused by the passage of an electric current and the production of electrical energy by chemical reactions.
  • Key concepts include oxidation-reduction reactions, electrochemical cells, and electrolysis.

2. Redox Reactions

  • Oxidation: Loss of electrons (increase in oxidation number)
  • Reduction: Gain of electrons (decrease in oxidation number)
  • A species that loses electrons is called a reducing agent (gets oxidized)
  • A species that gains electrons is called an oxidizing agent (gets reduced)
  • Redox reaction: A reaction where both oxidation and reduction occur simultaneously

3. Electrochemical Cells

  • Galvanic/Voltaic Cell: Converts chemical energy into electrical energy
  • Electrolytic Cell: Uses electrical energy to drive a non-spontaneous chemical reaction
  • Components of a Galvanic Cell:
    • Anode: The electrode where oxidation occurs (negative electrode)
    • Cathode: The electrode where reduction occurs (positive electrode)
    • Salt Bridge: Maintains electrical neutrality by allowing ion movement
    • External Circuit: Allows electron flow from anode to cathode

5. Electrochemical Series

  • The electrochemical series arranges elements according to their standard electrode potentials
  • More positive potential indicates higher tendency to be reduced (stronger oxidizing agent)
  • More negative potential indicates higher tendency to be oxidized (stronger reducing agent)
  • Elements at the top of the series can displace elements below them from their salt solutions

6. Electrolysis

  • Electrolysis is the process of using electricity to drive a non-spontaneous chemical reaction
  • Follows Faraday's Laws of Electrolysis:
    • First Law: The mass of a substance produced at an electrode is directly proportional to the quantity of electricity passed
    • Second Law: For the same quantity of electricity, the masses of different substances liberated at the electrodes are proportional to their chemical equivalents

7. Applications of Electrochemistry

  • Batteries: Devices that convert chemical energy to electrical energy
  • Fuel Cells: Continuously convert chemical energy to electrical energy
  • Electrolytic Refining: Purification of metals
  • Electroplating: Coating one metal with another using electrolysis
  • Corrosion: Electrochemical oxidation of metals in reaction with an oxidant

Tips for EAMCET

  • Remember the direction of electron flow: anode to cathode in galvanic cells
  • Practice calculating cell potentials and applying the Nernst equation
  • Understand the difference between galvanic and electrolytic cells
  • Memorize the electrochemical series for common elements
  • Know how to apply Faraday's laws for numerical problems
  • Familiarize yourself with common industrial applications of electrochemistry
