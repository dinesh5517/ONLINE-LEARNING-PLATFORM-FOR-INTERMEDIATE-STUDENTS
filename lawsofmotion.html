Laws of Motion - EAMCET Short Notes

1. First Law of Motion (Law of Inertia)

  • An object continues in a state of rest or uniform motion in a straight line unless acted upon by an external force.
  • Defines inertia as the resistance of any object to change its state of motion.
  • The measure of inertia is mass.
  • Examples: Passengers jerking forward when a moving bus stops suddenly; difficulty in moving a heavy object.

2. Second Law of Motion

  • The rate of change of momentum is directly proportional to the applied force and takes place in the direction of the force.
  • F = ma (Force = mass √ó acceleration)
  • 1 Newton: Force required to produce an acceleration of 1 m/s¬≤ in a body of mass 1 kg.
  • Applications: Rocket propulsion, recoil of a gun.

3. Third Law of Motion

  • For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
  • Action and reaction forces act on different bodies.
  • Examples: Walking, swimming, firing a bullet.
  • Force pairs are equal in magnitude but opposite in direction.

4. Law of Conservation of Momentum

  • When no external force acts on a system, the total momentum remains constant.
