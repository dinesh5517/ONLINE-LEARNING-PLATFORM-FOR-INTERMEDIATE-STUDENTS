Chemical Bonding - EAMCET Short Notes

1. Fundamentals of Chemical Bonding

  • Chemical bonding: Force that holds atoms together in molecules or compounds
  • Octet Rule: Atoms tend to gain/lose/share electrons to achieve 8 electrons in their valence shell
  • Types: Ionic, Covalent, Coordinate, Metallic, Hydrogen bonding
  • Bond energy: Energy required to break a chemical bond
Bond energy = Enthalpy change / Number of bonds broken
Bond length = Distance between nuclei of bonded atoms

2. Ionic Bonding

  • Transfer of electrons from metal to non-metal
  • Formation of cations and anions
  • Electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions
  • Properties: High melting/boiling points, conductivity in solution, brittle

4. Lewis Structures & VSEPR Theory

  • Lewis structures: Representation of valence electrons
  • Valence Shell Electron Pair Repulsion (VSEPR) theory predicts molecular geometry
  • Electron pair geometries: Linear, Trigonal Planar, Tetrahedral, Trigonal Bipyramidal, Octahedral
  • Lone pairs cause deviations in bond angles

6. Molecular Orbital Theory

  • Electrons in molecules occupy molecular orbitals (MOs)
  • Combination of atomic orbitals forms bonding and antibonding MOs
  • Bond order = ¬Ω(bonding e‚Åª - antibonding e‚Åª)
  • Bond order > 0: Stable molecule
  • Bond order = 0: Unstable molecule
  • Greater bond order = shorter, stronger bond

Tips for EAMCET

  • Focus on bond properties: length, energy, polarity, and angles
  • Remember the relationship between hybridization and geometry
  • Practice drawing Lewis structures for various molecules
  • Learn common examples of different types of bonds
  • Understand formal charge calculation and its significance
  • For polar molecules, remember that symmetrical molecules with polar bonds can be non-polar overall
  • Bond order correlates directly with bond strength and inversely with bond length
