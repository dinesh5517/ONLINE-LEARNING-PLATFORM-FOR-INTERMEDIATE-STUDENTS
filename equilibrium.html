Chemical Equilibrium - EAMCET Short Notes

1. Basic Concepts

  • Equilibrium: State where forward and reverse reaction rates are equal
  • Dynamic Nature: Reactions continue but concentrations remain constant
  • Characteristics:
    • Reversible reactions only
    • Closed system required
    • Macroscopic properties constant
    • Can be approached from either direction
  • Equilibrium in Physical Processes: Phase equilibria (solid-liquid-gas)

2. Law of Mass Action and Kc

  • Law of Mass Action: Rate ‚àù active masses of reactants

  • Features of Kc:
    • Constant at given temperature
    • Independent of initial concentrations
    • Pure solids/liquids excluded (activity = 1)
    • Depends on stoichiometric coefficients

4. Le Chatelier's Principle

  • Principle: System at equilibrium opposes any change in conditions
  • Effect of Concentration:
    • Increase reactant ‚Üí shift right
    • Increase product ‚Üí shift left
  • Effect of Pressure:
    • Increase P ‚Üí shifts to side with fewer gas moles
    • Decrease P ‚Üí shifts to side with more gas moles
  • Effect of Temperature:
    • Endothermic (ŒîH > 0): Increase T ‚Üí shift right
    • Exothermic (ŒîH < 0): Increase T ‚Üí shift left
  • Catalyst: No effect on equilibrium (speeds both directions equally)

6. Heterogeneous Equilibria

  • Key Concept: For the reaction CaCO3(s) ‚áå CaO(s) + CO2(g), the equilibrium constant is simply Kc = [CO2]
  • Why? Because:
    • Concentrations of pure solids (CaCO3 and CaO) are constant
    • Their "active masses" are incorporated into the equilibrium constant
    • Only gaseous or aqueous species appear in the expression
  • General Rule: Omit pure solids and liquids from equilibrium expressions

7. Relationship Between K and Reaction Quotient (Q)

  • Reaction Quotient (Q): Same form as K but calculated with initial concentrations
  • Comparison:
    Condition Relationship Direction of Shift
    System at equilibrium Q = K No shift
    Too much product Q > K ‚Üê Towards reactants
    Too much reactant Q < K ‚Üí Towards products

EAMCET Problem-Solving Tips

  • For Kc calculations, always check stoichiometric coefficients
  • Remember: Solids and pure liquids don't appear in equilibrium expressions
  • For Kp problems, pressure units must match R units (usually atm)
  • When temperature changes, K changes (use van't Hoff equation)
  • For Le Chatelier problems, identify the change first, then predict shift
  • Memorize common industrial processes (Haber, Contact, Ostwald)
  • For heterogeneous equilibria, only include gases and aqueous species
  • Quick K calculation: When coefficients are doubled, K becomes K¬≤
  • Reverse reaction: K' = 1/K
  • Adding reactions: Multiply their K values
