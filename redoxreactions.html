Redox Reactions - EAMCET Short Notes

1. Basic Concepts

  • Redox Reactions: Electron transfer reactions with both oxidation and reduction
  • Oxidation: Loss of electrons (increase in oxidation number)
  • Reduction: Gain of electrons (decrease in oxidation number)
  • Oxidizing Agent: Species that gets reduced (accepts electrons)
  • Reducing Agent: Species that gets oxidized (donates electrons)
Oxidation Half-Reaction:
Zn ‚Üí Zn2+ + 2e-
+
Reduction Half-Reaction:
Cu2+ + 2e- ‚Üí Cu
Complete Redox Reaction:
Zn + Cu2+ ‚Üí Zn2+ + Cu

2. Oxidation Numbers

  • Rules:
    1. Free elements: 0 (e.g., Na, O2)
    2. Monatomic ions: Equal to charge (Na+ = +1)
    3. Oxygen: Usually -2 (except peroxides: -1)
    4. Hydrogen: +1 with nonmetals, -1 with metals
    5. Fluorine: Always -1
    6. Sum equals charge of compound/ion
Species Oxidation States Example
KMnO4 K: +1, Mn: +7, O: -2 +1 +7 +4(-2) = 0
H2SO4 H: +1, S: +6, O: -2 2(+1) +6 +4(-2) = 0
Cr2O72- Cr: +6, O: -2 2(+6) +7(-2) = -2
Practice: Find oxidation state of S in Na2S2O3
2(+1) + 2x + 3(-2) = 0 ‚Üí x = +2

3. Balancing Redox Reactions

  • Half-Reaction Method (Ionic):
    1. Write separate oxidation and reduction half-reactions
    2. Balance atoms (except O and H)
    3. Balance O by adding H2O
    4. Balance H by adding H+ (acidic) or OH- (basic)
    5. Balance charge by adding electrons
    6. Equalize electrons in both half-reactions
    7. Add half-reactions and simplify
Example (Acidic): MnO4- + Fe2+ ‚Üí Mn2+ + Fe3+
Reduction: MnO4- + 8H+ + 5e- ‚Üí Mn2+ + 4H2O
Oxidation: Fe2+ ‚Üí Fe3+ + e- (√ó5)
Final: MnO4- + 5Fe2+ + 8H+ ‚Üí Mn2+ + 5Fe3+ + 4H2O

5. Electrochemical Cells

  • Galvanic Cell: Converts chemical ‚Üí electrical energy (spontaneous)
  • Electrolytic Cell: Converts electrical ‚Üí chemical energy (non-spontaneous)
  • Anode: Oxidation occurs (electrons leave)
  • Cathode: Reduction occurs (electrons enter)
  • Salt Bridge: Maintains electrical neutrality (ions flow)

7. Nernst Equation and Applications

  • Nernst Equation: Relates cell potential to concentration
    Ecell = E¬∞cell - (RT/nF)lnQ
    At 298K: Ecell = E¬∞cell - (0.0591/n)logQ
  • Applications:
    • Calculate cell potential under non-standard conditions
    • Determine equilibrium constants (when Ecell = 0)
    • Find concentration of solutions (pH meters)
Example Calculation:
For Zn|Zn2+(0.1M)||Cu2+(0.01M)|Cu (E¬∞ = 1.10V)
Q = [Zn2+]/[Cu2+] = 0.1/0.01 = 10
E = 1.10 - (0.0591/2)log10 = 1.10 - 0.0295 = 1.0705V

8. Faraday's Laws of Electrolysis

  • First Law: Mass deposited/liberated ‚àù quantity of electricity (Q = It)
  • Second Law: For same quantity of electricity, masses ‚àù equivalent weights
  • Formula:
    w = (MIt)/(nF)
    where:
    w = mass deposited (g)
    M = molar mass (g/mol)
    I = current (A)
    t = time (s)
    n = moles of electrons
    F = Faraday's constant (96485 C/mol)
Example: Calculate mass of Ag deposited when 2A flows for 30min through AgNO3 solution
Ag+ + e- ‚Üí Ag (n=1)
t = 30 √ó 60 = 1800s
w = (107.87 √ó 2 √ó 1800)/(1 √ó 96485) ‚âà 4.02g

9. Corrosion and Prevention

  • Corrosion: Redox reaction of metals with environment (e.g., rusting of iron)
  • Mechanism:
    1. Anode (Oxidation): Fe ‚Üí Fe2+ + 2e-
    2. Cathode (Reduction): O2 + 2H2O + 4e- ‚Üí 4OH-
    3. Product formation: Fe2+ + 2OH- ‚Üí Fe(OH)2
    4. Further oxidation: 4Fe(OH)2 + O2 ‚Üí 2Fe2O3¬∑3H2O (rust)
  • Prevention Methods:
    • Galvanization (Zn coating)
    • Sacrificial protection (Mg or Zn anodes)
    • Alloying (stainless steel)
    • Painting/greasing
    • Cathodic protection
