2. IUPAC Nomenclature

  • Steps:
    1. Identify longest carbon chain (parent hydrocarbon)
    2. Number to give lowest locants to substituents
    3. Name substituents alphabetically
  • Prefixes: meth- (1), eth- (2), prop- (3), but- (4), etc.

5. Types of Organic Reactions

  • Substitution: One group replaces another (SN¬π, SN¬≤)
  • Addition: Atoms added to double/triple bonds (Markovnikov's rule)
  • Elimination: Removal of atoms (E1, E2 - Saytzeff rule)
  • Rearrangement: Migration of atom/group (carbocation stability)
  • Oxidation-Reduction: Change in oxidation state

7. Important Concepts

  • Markovnikov's Rule: "The rich get richer" - H adds to carbon with more H's
  • Saytzeff Rule: Forms more substituted alkene in elimination
  • Huckel's Rule: 4n+2 œÄ electrons for aromaticity
  • Chirality: Optical isomers (enantiomers) rotate plane-polarized light
  • Resonance: Delocalization of œÄ electrons (stabilizes molecule)
